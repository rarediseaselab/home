CiliaAtlas: Cell Ciliation Database

CiliaAtlas is a curated atlas classifying 359 human cell types across 13 organ systems and 83 organs by ciliation status. Each entry documents whether a cell type bears motile, primary, nodal, modified, or no cilia, with axonemal structure, cilia function, confidence level, and PubMed-linked evidence. CiliaAtlas is part of the Multidimensional Ciliary Atlas (MCA) project.

This resource allows researchers to:

  • Browse 359 human cell types classified by ciliation status across 13 organ systems
  • Filter by organ system, ciliation status, cilia type, and confidence level
  • Access PubMed evidence directly via linked PMIDs
  • Download the full atlas or any filtered subset as a CSV file
CiliaAtlas supports both basic and clinical researchers investigating ciliary biology, ciliopathies, and the cellular basis of rare diseases.

📖 How to Use CiliaAtlas
  • Search: Enter any cell type name, organ, or organ system — results update instantly.
  • Filter: Use the dropdowns to narrow by organ system, ciliation status, cilia type, or confidence level.
  • Reset: Click Reset All to clear all filters and reload the full atlas.
  • PubMed links: Each PMID in the last column is a direct link to the PubMed record.
  • Download: Export the full atlas or your current filtered view as a CSV file.
  • Ciliation statuses:
    • Ciliated – Primary: Single non-motile 9+0 axoneme; sensory and signalling roles
    • Ciliated – Motile: Multiple motile 9+2 cilia; fluid movement and mucociliary clearance
    • Ciliated – Nodal: Transient motile cilia; left-right embryonic axis determination
    • Ciliated – Modified: Highly specialised cilia derivatives (e.g. photoreceptor outer segment)
    • Context-Dependent: Ciliation varies by developmental stage, activation state, or culture condition
    • Non-Ciliated: No cilia reported in current literature
    • Unclassified – Review Needed: Insufficient or conflicting evidence; manual curation required
