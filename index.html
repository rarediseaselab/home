About

Oktay I. Kaplan is an Associate Professor and Principal Investigator who leads the Rare Disease Laboratory (the Kaplan Lab) in the Faculty of Life and Natural Sciences at Abdullah Gül University in Kayseri, Türkiye. The group studies ciliopathies, rare genetic diseases, and neurodevelopmental disorders, combining the nematode Caenorhabditis elegans with CRISPR/Cas9 genome editing and bioinformatics.

A central focus of the lab is interpreting the functional consequences of patient-derived genetic variants. By introducing patient variants into the orthologous C. elegans genes through CRISPR/Cas9 knock-in, the group models candidate disease alleles in a living animal and measures their effects on cilium structure, intraflagellar transport, and sensory behaviour. This worm-based readout complements clinical genetics and helps assess variants of uncertain significance.

Alongside experimental work, the lab develops open bioinformatics resources for the cilia and rare-disease research communities and maintains long-standing collaborations with clinical geneticists and cell biologists in Türkiye and internationally.

Research tools & databases

The lab builds and maintains a suite of open resources for ciliary genetics:

CiliaHub / CiliaAI

Curated catalogue of human ciliary genes with an AI-assisted query interface.

CiliaAtlas

Localisation and expression reference for cilia-associated proteins.

CilioGenics

Integrated method and database for predicting novel ciliary genes (Nucleic Acids Research, 2024).

CiliaMiner

Manually curated database of ciliopathy genes and ciliopathies (Database, 2023).

ConVarT

Cross-species comparison of genetic variants for variant interpretation.
