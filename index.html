Research

Research Overview

Our lab is dedicated to uncovering the genetic and molecular mechanisms driving rare diseases, with a particular focus on ciliopathies. By integrating comparative genomics, functional assays, and cutting-edge gene editing technologies, we aim to bridge the gap between genotype and phenotype.

Cilia & Ciliopathies

We study Joubert syndrome, Bardet-Biedl syndrome, and cranioectodermal dysplasia to identify causative genes.

Read More

Disease Modeling

Using CRISPR/Cas9-based functional screening in C. elegans, we distinguish disease-causing variants.

Read More

Cilia-Cilia Joining

We investigate the molecular machinery involved in cilia-cilia elongation and inter-ciliary communication.

Read More

Bioinformatics

We develop and maintain open bioinformatics tools — CiliaHub, CiliaAtlas, CilioGenics, and CiliaMiner.
