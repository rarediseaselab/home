Resources

Our lab is committed to open science. We develop and freely share bioinformatics tools, databases, and experimental protocols to accelerate research in ciliopathies and rare diseases.

🧬
CiliaHub / CiliaAI — Curated database of 2,002+ experimentally confirmed ciliary genes; an evolution of the ciliary "Gold Standard" gene list. Developed by the Kaplan Lab.
Open CiliaHub in-page →  |  theciliahub.github.io
🔬
CiliaAtlas — Cilia cell atlas classifying 359 human cell types by ciliation status across 13 organ systems. Developed by the Kaplan Lab.
Open CiliaAtlas →  |  rarediseaselab.github.io/home/ciliaatlas.html
🔭
CilioGenics — Integrated database and computational tool for predicting novel ciliary genes. Developed by the Kaplan Lab.
ciliogenics.com
📊
CiliaMiner — Bioinformatics platform for exploring ciliopathy genes and ciliopathy-disease associations. Developed by the Kaplan Lab.
kaplanlab.shinyapps.io/ciliaminer
🔗
ConVarT — Search engine for matching human genetic variants with orthologous variants from non-human species. Developed by the Kaplan Lab.
convart.org  |  Source code on GitHub
💻
GitHub — Open-source code, pipelines, and repositories for all lab tools.
github.com/thekaplanlab  |  github.com/rarediseaselab
🧪
CRISPR/Cas9 Protocol — PCR-based, cost-efficient screening strategy for C. elegans functional studies.
